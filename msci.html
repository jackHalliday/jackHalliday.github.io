Experimental investigation of radiatively driven plasma flows

In this project we'll be investigating plasma flows which are driven by the X-Ray burst from an imploding wire-array Z-Pinch. These plasma flows are fast (V ~ 40 km/s); hot (T ~ 2e5 K); and dense (n ~ 1e24 /m^3). They expand into strong magnetic fields (B ~ 10 T), which are driven by the current flowing through the Z-pinch. A self-emission image of a typical experiment is shown below.

This is an experimental configuration which is highly relevant to applications in inertial confinement fusion (ICF) and laboratory astrophysics. Our focus in the project will likely be on the laboratory astrophysics side, however if you have a strong interest in ICF then there is some flexibility here. Laboratory astrophysics involves creating small scale plasma flows which can be scaled up to predict the behaviour of some astrophysical object. This is an extension of the process which is used in aeronautics to scale between small models of aircraft in wind tunnels to the real thing.

The project will involve experimental work on the MAGPIE pulsed power generator. This is a unique experimental facility housed in the basement of the Blackett building which is capable of producing mega-ampere current pulses over ~ 100 ns timescales. The conditions of the plasmas in the experiments we perform will be measured using a variety of state-of-the art diagnostic techniques including laser-interferometry; time-gated self-emission imaging; and Thomson scattering. During the project you will gain experience working with these diagnostics and be guided through the process of analysing experimental data.

If your ability to work in the lab is disrupted by COVID at any point during the year, then we are in a position to accommodate this however the focus will necessarily pivot from hands-on work to data analysis.

The project will be supervised by Sergey Lebedev and Jack Halliday. If you're an Imperial physics MSci student and this proposal interests you, please email jack.halliday12@imperial.ac.uk to discuss further.
